“AI neobank” is 90% narrative. We tagged the 10% that isn’t.

We audited all 368 neobanks for AI actually in production. 67 passed. Most marketing didn’t.

Every neobank pitch deck this year says "AI-powered." So we did the boring thing: we went through all 368 entities in the directory, name by name, and checked which ones have AI verifiably in production — against SEC filings, regulator disclosures, annual reports, engineering blogs, even a peer-reviewed NLP paper.

67 of 368 passed. The rest is roadmap, waitlist, or vibes.

The three places AI actually lives in a bank

Underwriting — 39 entities. The oldest and least glamorous bucket, and the only one with a decade of receipts. Nubank, Kaspi, WeBank, MyBank, Branch, FairMoney: machine-learning credit models on alternative data, disclosed in filings because they're material to the business. If AI disappeared tomorrow, these loan books would notice. That's the test.

Interface — 21 entities. The AI assistant is the product surface: Cleo-style money chat, Bunq's Finn, Klarna's service assistant (the one doing the workload of ~700 human agents, per their own disclosure). Harder to verify than underwriting — we required a shipped, generally-available feature, not a beta toggle.

Agentic — 7 entities. The genuinely new segment: banking built for AI agents as customers — agent-issued cards, spend controls for autonomous software, stablecoin rails for machine-to-machine payments. It's tiny and mostly crypto-native, and it's the one to watch: every previous wave of neobanking started as a weird seven-entity corner of the directory.

What didn't survive verification

The interesting list is who we didn't tag. First drafts had ~80 candidates; a dozen got cut when the "AI feature" turned out to be:

  • a waitlist page with a GPT-wrapper demo
  • a press release about a "strategic AI partnership" with no shipped product
  • a chatbot that is a decision tree from 2019 wearing a new name
  • claims made by third-party listicles that the company itself never made

The deleted tags matter more than the applied ones. If a company you know should be tagged (or shouldn't), the tag and its source are on every profile — reply and we'll re-verify.

Why this is a field in the dataset now

ai: underwriting | interface | agentic is now a first-class field in data.json, filterable on the homepage, and documented for the LLM crawlers in llms.txt. When the "AI bank" narrative peaks, you'll be able to diff who actually shipped.
