We finished the licence census: 368 neobanks, zero unclassified

For the first time, every entity in the directory has a documented regulatory setup. Here’s the map.

For months the neobankbeat directory had a dirty little bucket: "Unclassified." 173 entities where we hadn't yet verified who actually licenses, sponsors or charters the thing. This week we cleared it. Every one of the 368 tracked neobanks now has a documented regulatory setup, verified against regulator registers, filings and disclosures.

Three readings of that chart

1. "Neobanks aren't banks" is two-thirds true. 127 of 368 hold a real charter — more than the folk wisdom says (the number jumped from our old estimate of 93 once we classified everything), but still a minority. And the licensed cohort skews heavily toward Asia's purpose-built digital-bank licence regimes — Singapore, Hong Kong, the Philippines, Saudi Arabia, Korea hand out charters designed for this; the US essentially doesn't.

2. The partner-bank model is the biggest single point of failure. 77 entities — including some of the largest US names — exist at the pleasure of a sponsor bank. Fi Money had 3.5 million customers when Federal Bank ended the relationship in March. The customers kept their accounts. Fi didn't keep its customers.

Scale and charter are uncorrelated: GCash and MoMo — two of the biggest "neobanks" on earth by users — are e-money issuers, not banks.

3. The census surfaced the corpses. Verifying 173 regulatory setups means reading a lot of regulator notices, and five entities turned out to be dead or absorbed (last issue covered them). A licence census doubles as a pulse check.

Things we learned along the way

  • Rocker (Sweden) handed back its own payment-institution licence in 2025 and now operates under another bank's charter — de-licensing as a cost decision.
  • Nickel (France) runs 4M+ accounts through tobacco shops on a payment institution licence — the most successful "not a bank" in Europe.
  • A Pakistani ROSCA app, a Panamanian e-money issuer and a Solana smart-contract account don't fit any standard bucket. That's what "Other / mixed" is for — we'd rather have an honest 9-entity bucket than force a wrong label.

Every profile page now states the specific setup — not just the class, but the actual licence, register number or partner bank, with sources.
