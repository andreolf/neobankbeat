We deleted five neobanks from the directory this week

Juno, Kard, Z1, Fi Money, Pomelo. Different countries, different products, same cause of death.

This week the neobankbeat directory went from 373 entities to 368. Not because we got stricter — because five neobanks stopped existing. Here's the obituary column, and the one pattern that runs through all of it.

The five

Juno (US) — the crypto-friendly checking account. Its BaaS middleware, Synapse, collapsed in 2024 and froze customer funds across 50+ fintechs. Juno spent two years trying to dig out. In September 2025 it published a farewell letter instead. Cause of death: someone else's ledger.

Kard (France) — the teen banking app, 200,000 users, self-described leader of its segment. Its e-money provider Okali (a Crédit Agricole subsidiary) unilaterally terminated the contract in April 2025, citing AML compliance. Liquidation followed in September; the app went dark in January 2026. Cause of death: someone else's licence.

Z1 (Brazil) — the Gen Z account. Absorbed into NG.CASH in February 2025; the brand no longer exists. Cause of death: consolidation — the polite one.

Fi Money (India) — the most pedigreed of the five. Founded by the ex-Google Pay India team, $168M raised, 3.5M customers. In March 2026 its partner Federal Bank ended the relationship as a "business re-alignment" and pushed every customer to the bank's own app. Fi's consumer product ceased to exist in four sentences of an email the bank wrote. Cause of death: someone else's charter.

Pomelo (US) — the credit card built for remittances. Acquired by Zepz in January 2026; product "temporarily paused" with no relaunch date. We don't track pauses. Cause of death: acqui-shutdown.

The pattern

Three of the five — Juno, Kard, Fi Money — died the same way: the layer underneath them flipped a switch. Not fraud, not a hack, not a bank run. A provider, an EMI, a partner bank deciding the relationship was no longer worth the compliance overhead.

When you don't control the layer you live on, your continuity is a line item in someone else's risk review.

We just finished classifying the regulatory setup of every single one of the 368 entities we track (next issue is the full map). The headline: only 127 hold a full banking licence. The other 241 rent — partner banks, e-money licences, BIN sponsors, card issuers. Every rental agreement is a kill switch.

Why we delete instead of archive

A directory that keeps dead entries isn't a directory, it's a graveyard with good SEO. Every entity on neobankbeat is verified active — that's the whole promise. When one dies, we verify the death (court filings, farewell letters, regulator notices — all five above are sourced) and remove it, and every count on the site updates.

If you spot a zombie we've missed, reply to this email. Deletions matter as much as additions.
