The 107 companies your neobank actually runs on

We mapped the infrastructure layer — BIN sponsors, BaaS platforms, core banking, stablecoin rails — under every neobank we track.

Your neobank has a beautiful app. Underneath it: a sponsor bank you've never heard of, a card processor, a core-banking vendor, a KYC provider, maybe a stablecoin rail. When a neobank dies (see: two issues ago), it usually dies at one of those layers.

So we mapped it. The new infra section documents 107 infrastructure providers and links each to the neobanks in the directory that verifiably run on it.

Three things the map makes obvious

1. Concentration is the norm, not the exception. Coastal Community Bank alone sponsors a meaningful chunk of US consumer fintech. All 35 web3-native card programs we track sit on a handful of BIN sponsors. One compliance decision at one sponsor propagates to a dozen brands overnight — the WaveCrest topology of 2018, one layer more professional.

When the next Synapse happens, you should be able to look up the blast radius in one click. Now you can.

2. The provider layer has its own graveyard. Synapse (bankrupt, ~$200M frozen), Wirecard Card Solutions (FCA freeze), Railsr (restructured), Solaris (recapitalized under pressure), Okali (terminated Kard into liquidation). Diligence your infra like it's a counterparty — because it is.

3. "Get close to the metal" is measurable. The neobanks that survived a decade either hold their own charter (127 of 368 do) or own their processing stack. The ones that rent everything are one contract termination away from a farewell letter — we removed three of those this month.

Who's on the map

  • 24 BIN sponsor banks — Coastal Community, Sutton, Lead Bank, Celtic, Cross River in the US; Federal Bank and SBM in India; Okali and Treezor in France. The banks that lend fintechs their right to issue cards.
  • 15 card-issuing processors — Marqeta, Pomelo (the Argentine one — no relation to the deceased US card), Rain, Baanx.
  • 13 core-banking platforms — Mambu, Thought Machine, 10x, and the vendors nobody tweets about.
  • 10 BaaS / e-money platforms — the middleware layer. This is where Synapse lived.
  • 16 crypto-card + stablecoin infrastructure providers — the newest shelf, and the fastest growing.

Each provider page lists type, HQ, what it actually does, and the tracked neobanks running on it.
